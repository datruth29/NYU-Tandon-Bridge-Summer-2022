$T(N) = o(p(N))$ if, for all positive constants $c$, there exists an $n_0$ such that $T(N) < cp(N)$ when $N$ > $n_0$.

Less formally, $T(N) = o(p(N))$ if $T(N) = O(p(N))$ and $T(N) \neq \Theta(p(N))$

The growth rate of $T(N)$ is __less than__ the growth rate of $p(N)$.
