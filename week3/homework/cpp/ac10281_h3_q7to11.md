### 3.2.4: A subset of a power set.

#### B) Let $A=\{1, 2, 3\}$. What is $\{X \isin P(A): 2 \isin X\}$?

This is stating that $X$ is the subsets of all sets in the $P(A)$ where $2$ is an element in that subset.

In which case, the subsets would be $X=\{\{2\}, \{1, 2\}, \{2, 3\}, \{1, 2, 3\}\}$

### 3.3.1: Unions and intersections of sets.

Define the sets A, B, C, and D as follows:

$
\begin{aligned}
    A &= \{-3, 0, 1, 4, 17\}\\
    B &= \{-12, -5, 1, 4, 6\}\\
    C &= \{x \isin \mathbb{Z}: \text{ x is odd}\}\\
    D &= \{x \isin \mathbb{Z}: \text{ x is positive}\}\\
\end{aligned}
$

#### C) $A \cap C$

$
A \cap C = \{-3, 1, 17\}
$

#### D) $A \cup ( B \cap C)$

$
A \cup ( B \cap C) = \{-5, -3 , 0, 1, 4, 17\}
$

#### E) $A \cap B \cap C$

$
A \cap B \cap C = \{1\}
$

### 3.3.3: Unions and intersections of sequences of sets, part 2

Use the following definitions to express each union or intersection given. You can use roster or set builder notation in your responses, but no set operations. For each definition, $i \isin \mathbb{Z^+}$

$
\begin{aligned}
    A_i &= \{i^0, i^1, i^2\}\\
    B_i &= \{x \isin \mathbb{R}: -i \leq x \leq 1/i\}\\
    C_i &= \{x \isin \mathbb{R}: -1/i \leq x \leq 1/i\}\\
\end{aligned}
$

#### A) $\bigcap_{i=2}^{5}Ai$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\bigcap_{i=2}^{5}Ai &= \{2^0, 2^1, 2^2\} \cap \{3^0, 3^1, 3^2\} \cap \{4^0, 4^1,4^2\} \cap \{5^0, 5^1, 5^2\} \\
&= \{1\}
\end{aligned}
$$

#### B) $\bigcup_{i=2}^{5}Ai$

$$
\begin{aligned}
    \bigcup_{i=2}^{5}Ai &= \{2^0, 2^1, 2^2\} \cup \{3^0, 3^1, 3^2\} \cup \{4^0, 4^1,4^2\} \cup \{5^0, 5^1, 5^2\} \\
    &= \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 9, 16, 25\}
\end{aligned}
$$

#### E) $\bigcap_{i=1}^{100}Ci$

$$
\bigcap_{i=1}^{100}Ci = \{x \isin \mathbb{R}: -1/100 \leq x \leq 1/100\}
$$

#### F) $\bigcup_{i=1}^{100}Ci$

$$
\bigcup_{i=1}^{100}Ci = \{x \isin \mathbb{R}: -1 \leq x \leq 1\}
$$
